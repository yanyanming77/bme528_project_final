Introduction

Background
  • Radiation therapy can irradiate tumors to destroy the cancerous cells
  • Radiation Therapy requires optimal dose to balance between destroying the cancer tumor target while preserving surrounding healthy tissue
  • Outcomes of patients with similar clinical and treatment characteristics remains heterogeneous, with large variations observed for both disease outcomes and treatment toxicity.
  • The clinical workflow in RT includes time-consuming iterations of planning algorithms
  • Recent development in oncologic biomarkers has spurred interest in the use of personalized, risk-adapted approaches to cancer treatment.
Motivation
  • Historical clinical data can be a source of knowledge to provide evidence-based decision support for future patients
  • A system that can retrieve historical similar cases to assist clinicians in planning treatment and provide quantified empirical estimates to help optimize radiation dose is needed
Our Goal

Our main purpose is to develop a web-based decision support platform and the GUI to provide consistency pre-and-after treatment.
